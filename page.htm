When people call Kanyes music experimental or genius theyre usually just trying to cover up the fact that the music sucks- but The Life of Pablo legitimately is experimental genius.
Theres so much insane shit on this album and like 80% of it works, which considering how out-there this album gets, is fucking remarkable.
Pt. 2, Feedback, Freestyle 4, these are songs that on paper sound like they would be shit, but theyre all fucking great, ESPECIALLY Pt. 2, I fuckin love that song.
But the true centerpieces of this album to me are as follows:
Ultralight Beam - Pretty self-explanatory. Beautiful enchanting gospel greatness with Chance the Rapper delivering one of the great rap verses in history.
Father Stretch My Hands Pt. 1 - One of Kid Cudis greatest hooks ever, and one of the most effortlessly fun vibe songs ever recorded. The asshole bleach line is very good lol.
Famous - This song is fun, but it doesnt reach true transcendence until the outro with one of the all-time best beat switches in rap history. Like- holy fucking shit. It really can just ride that beat switch with *insert whoever it is yelling lol* goin LETS FUCKIN GOOO KANYE WEST for like a full minute and it stays hype the entire time just because of how incredible it is.
Real Friends - One of the real Kanye classics, with some great verses over a strikingly minimal and phenomenal beat. It really feels like an updated 2016 version of Late Reg Kanye in a way we really havent heard on anything post-Graduation since Gorgeous and maybe like one other song Im forgetting.
30 Hours - The beat on this song is so fucking good that Kanye can spend half the song going this the bonus track and it doesnt matter at all. Its an insane vibe. DEN I MADE MYSELF A SMOOOOOOTHIE.
Fade - The bass grooves. Dude. Holy. Fuck.
Saint Pablo - This is Kanyes best song, fuck you. The beat is perfect and Kanyes verses are fucking amazing, except maybe the jew line lol. (Btw- Thank God for Jay Electronica, he down wit the mission- lmfao) I love how you can hear him choking up when he says real family stick together and see through the mirage. When I first listened to this song, it really felt like my life fucking changed. Samphas parts on this song blow my fuckin shit every time. One of the few rap songs that never fails to make me cry.

Overall, The Life of Pablo is really good and I like it and Im bad at writing reviews. Sorry lol.

what does one do when their dreams come true?

after the college trilogy [the early 2000s pop rap glitz mostly resulted in these sliding off my gen-z ears as 'dated', though graduation's highlights certainly managed to make an impact], the influential interlude of 808s, the critical behemoth of mbdtf, and yeezus virulent digital-edged thunderhead of superego [not in the freudian sense but rather a adjectivized noun to describe kanye's ego] leaving us with the life of pablo.

wherein; kanye reckons with the realization that he is in fact a small blip on the face of an immensely large planet as opposed to the inverse. facing pressure from all sides, be it music, fashion, family, you name it, kanye finds himself overwhelmed.

i ask again, what does one do when their dreams come true? in particular, what does one do when their dreams have come true, and yet they are surrounded on all sides by temptation, greed, money, and literally anything the mind and wealth could possibly conjure? the weight of the world finally begins to press upon the superzepplin of kanye's ballooning profile, and kanye is forced to recognize that sheer arrogance cannot overcome all things, though he still damn well tries. questions and contradictions arise- for instance, how does one create a working marriage and subsequent family when one is a hopeless womanizer? how does one continue to innovate when the circle of those closest is infiltrated and fractured by onlookers with ill intentions? when one feels despite the number of people in the room the only person they can trust is themselves? burning ambition begins to lose direction as the number of conceivable milestones dwindles, signaling the beginning of the end.

despite the grim circumstances that prompt the lucidity displayed on this album, it truly manifests in some of the greatest songs kanye has ever recorded. ultralight beam's hymnal triumph yet grim acknowledgement of the path that lies ahead. father stretch my hands' prodigal production and impassioned delivery. the heartfelt prognoses of fml and real friends, the bareness of 30 hours, the hip hop masterpiece of no more parties in la, and the perfect bow of saint pablo- it makes the spiral since all the more tragic.

beyond the album itself, i firmly believes that this marks simultaneously the glorious pinnacle as well as the end of kanye's winning streak, perhaps the point at which the last glimmers of groundedness leave him.

spoiler: click to read fuck vultures by the way. barring a miraculous shift, kanye is finished.

A Broken Man Stuck Between Change and Conformity
The Life of Pablo is a mess of an album, theres no other way to put it, from the background behind its release to the lawsuit over the false advertising behind the exclusive streaming rights to the labelling of the record being a living breathing changing creative expression, the fact this was released in any form other than a leak is kind of astonishing. The Life of Pablo focuses on the main theme Ive been finding throughout Kanyes last few albums, his struggle with himself and his stance in the media as a public figure, and it approaches it in such a scattershot fashion. The final version of the album has 20 songs and spans a bit over an hour long but feels so much longer despite being about as long as the first two Kanye albums, the songs and the way they flow into each other feels whiplash inducing but a lot of them carry the same sound and, even eight years after its release, the mixing still kind of sucks, but even though these are huge flaws that would make any other album worse, theres something about Pablo that makes these flaws endearing.

By The Life of Pablos release, Kanye was a father and attempting to rediscover his faith, and this album tackles the idea of who Ye wants to be, a family man aligning himself with God or the same man hes been for the last few years. The album cover is this total mess of text with the phrase Which one? plastered among photos of a wedding and somebodys ass, pretty clearly showing what Pablo represents, and the albums messiness feels like a sonic reflection of that. MBDTF showed his struggles with the media, Yeezus showed his struggles with his ego and the idea of others accepting him for who he is, Pablo shows his inner struggle with what Kanye wants to be, you have the opener Ultralight Beam which is this reflective gospel rap song revolving around a skeletal production as Chance The Rapper and Kelly Price speak about their faith and where its put them in life and then you have Father Stretch My Hands, Pt. 1 which is a colossally fun stadium banger where Kanye raps about a bleached asshole, Low Lights samples a track where Sandy Rivera pours her heart about her commitment to God and Highlights is another fun club song with the line I need every bad bitch up in Equinox, I need to know right now if youre a freak or not, Pablo is contradictory in nature. Themes and the way they influence an album are cool and all and do lend Pablo a lot of praise but I still find a lot of issues with the sound. The production here honestly blows at some points, the mixing of all the elements in the mix on Highlights and Waves and Fade doesnt feel polished, the beats are either really goddamn great (Father Stretch My hands, Pt. 1, 30 Hours, No More Parties in LA, Saint Pablo) or eh (the first half of Famous, FML, Facts (Charlie Heat Version)) and Kanye just sounds bad. Its also a bit of a problem that I dont really dig many of the bars here, there are a lot of funny and notable ones like the bleached asshole line or the one about the sandwich on Wolves, but theres only four or so songs here where I can say I like the song for Kanyes presence more than the beat or a feature. I think a lot of the middle of the album is kind of boring, I dont dig Highlights, Waves or Freestyle 4 and FML is kind of boring outside of The Weeknds hook, all the great songs on here are in the first or last third, and those songs are great to be fair.

Feedback benefits from being a Ye-only song with a pretty good beat and great flow, Pt. 2 is literally just Panda by Desiigner but it works somehow, Real Friends has Kanye talking about how none of his friends feel like theyre there for him and only there because hes Kanye as well as referencing a cousin who stole his laptop and made him pay $250,000 with a great minimal beat and guest verse from Ty Dolla $ign, I think Wolves is alright and has grown off of me a lot but Franks Track, a verse originally on Wolves by Frank Ocean that Kanye removed and made its own track, has a nice little atmosphere to it, Fade has a really great beat, Facts (Charlie Heat Version) is just pretty fun even if I dont feel too strongly about the verses or beat, 30 Hours is over five minutes long with half of it just being the same beat and vocal samples looped as Kanye ad-libs and takes a phone call but its one of the best songs on the entire album, the beat is incredible, Kanyes verse is really damn good and the hook is cool, No More Parties in LA has a phenomenal beat from Madlib alongside what might be the best verses Kanye has ever delivered, he is absolutely incredible on this track and so is Kendrick Lamar who also does a stellar job but the mixing on his vocals is distractingly bad and closer Saint Pablo is an incredible way for the album to end, detailing Kanyes relationship with himself, his wife and the media as clearly as the album can get, talking about how his debt was used as a way for articles to call him crazy and reckless with a great beat and stellar hook from Sampha.

In a lot of ways, I think The Life of Pablo is heavily flawed, as much as Id like to preach the albums themes of Kanyes struggle with himself, a lot of the record doesnt hold impact to those themes or even explores them. Its all over the place and unfinished, it feels like a leak more than an actual full-length album that Kanye put out, if this was more fleshed out and more streamlined then it couldve been really great but instead it falls short of it. I do think theres greatness within The Life of Pablo and Kanyes struggles with the two lives do make for interesting and compelling music, its just a bit unfortunate that the place where these themes shine brightest is everything relating to the album besides the music. The most unfortunate part of The Life of Pablo though? This is the last good Kanye West album.

I Feel Like Pablo
By 2016, the modern ills of Kanye West began to show more prominently than ever. With everything from his hospitalization to the endorsement of Donald Trump, Kanye West was in a more volatile state than ever. The Life of Pablo really puts this mental state on display in more ways than one. Not only was this his most manic, chaotic, and disjointed release to date, but it was also one of his better experiments. The Life of Pablo combines the aesthetics of his last 2 records (Yeezus and My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy) while simultaneously taking on a quieter and more vulnerable tone. The immense egoism of the early 2010s is present but not prevalent. While not his best, The Life of Pablo is a tastefully subdued, well-crafted, and moderately experimental experience in his discography that shows flashes of his trademark personality.

The first 5 tracks of this album are one of Kanye West's best album introductions in his entire discography. They, along with others, kick off the record with what would make up about 50% of the vibe of this album. This is where the egoism and bombastic antics of My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy combine with the crushing production of Yeezus. As the album progresses, we get some more traditional and subdued cuts akin to the albums of his early career (The College Dropout). The Life of Pablo really feels like a musical and personal recollection of Kanye West's career up until this point. All the songs on the album have great beats, familiarly witty lyrics, and great aesthetics. This is a totally solid release in Kanye West's storied discography.

WHICH / ONE will be The Life of Pablo.

Incredibly challenging in all the ways one wants a piece of art to be
It took about half of it's run time before I finally "got" The Life of Pablo. I wasn't understanding the artistic intentions behind it until "Wolves" gave me a clue. By the end of "30 Hours," I was sold.

This album conveys feelings of confusion and alienation masterfully. The infamous album cover art should clue you in. It's disjointed, lost, eagerly indecisive, perplexed, turbulent, and agitated. These are all different ways to experience confusion and they're all experienced on the album.

Kanye West has always been a favorite musical artist of mine. (The way he unfortunately tarnished his own public image aside.) But, I never really considered him a "genius" or "one of the greatest artist in modern times." Those weren't important to me and I'm not sure they are even now. But, I think it's important to talk about how The Life of Pablo resembles what is typically considered "high art." That is, art that isn't afraid that it'll go unappreciated by most people.

The album is enigmatic and eclectic. There's a lot to listen to and a lot to "miss" in a way that elements of it go unappreciated. Even now, I don't consider the album flawless. But, by accepting that it will have perceived flaws for some listeners, TLOP is able to carve out a firmly-held space in the cultural consciousness.

There are moments that are executed in inspiring fashion. There's so much excitement and emotion in the music and the way its performed.

Still, the album is very, deeply challenging. It covers a lot of musical bases. It touches on a lot of themes. Not everyone will like everything. The album is especially greater than the sum of its individual songs.

But, by intentionally and masterfully creating disagreement in its own reception, TLOP encourages us to talk about our varied experiences with music. It's fascinating.

Artists, take note: this is how you do an album about confusion without being pretentious or disorientating.

Personal impact on me

I'm anguishing over whether to give this album a perfect score or a near-perfect score. I narrowly had this album go completely over my head. As an experience, The Life of Pablo is one of the most interesting music has to offer. It's a complete album experience.

Now that I understand the project, I think the songs on here will only continue to grow on me. Even now, I can look back on the beginning songs with a greater appreciation.

Still, I think Kanye has shown me I listen to music not as a high art form. But as something to emotionally connect with more than intellectually. It's more important for me to be able to dance to a song than to recognize its artistic value. But, for showing me that, I am extremely grateful.
